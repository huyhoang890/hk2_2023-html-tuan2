Preserving Your Memories

Scrapbook albums have existed since the beginning of photography. However, the sad fact is that photographs and most printed material are not permanent: they will fade and yellow with age. Scrapbookers of today are aware of these problems, and the insdustry is providing remedies to minimize deterioration. For the best results, avoid using materials with high acid content which can cause photos and paper to deteriorate. Another thing to avoid is lignin, a material that is the bonding element in wood fiber. Over time, paper with lignin will become yelow and brittle, so you should only use lignin-free products.


Your albums should contain page protectors to shield the pages from smudges, oil, and dirt that can be transferred form your hands. You should never use albums with sticky "magnetic" pages. The sticky substance will be transferred to the photo and backing paper casing deterioration. Never crop Polaroid ® photos: thay will curl and fall apart. Mount all memorabilia on acid-free caredstock paper, and photocopy all newspaper clippings on acid-free paper.
